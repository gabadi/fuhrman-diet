## Part 1: Core Principle (H=N/C)

### The Guideline
**H = N/C** (Health = Nutrients / Calories)

Your health is predicted by your nutrient intake divided by your caloric intake. Maximize micronutrient density per calorie consumed.

### The Scientific Justification

#### Why This Formula Matters

The H=N/C equation is not arbitrary - it reflects fundamental biological reality:

1. **Micronutrients vs Macronutrients**: Your body needs two categories of nutrients:
   - **Macronutrients** (carbs, protein, fat) provide calories/energy
   - **Micronutrients** (vitamins, minerals, phytochemicals) do NOT provide calories but are essential for all cellular functions, repair, and disease prevention

2. **The Critical Insight**: When you consume food low in micronutrients but high in calories (processed foods, oils, refined grains), you:
   - Get excess calories that promote weight gain and metabolic dysfunction
   - Create cellular deficiency in protective compounds
   - Accelerate aging through oxidative damage and glycation

3. **Why Micronutrient Density Predicts Health**:
   > "When you eat mostly high-nutrient foods, the body ages slower and is armed to prevent and reverse many common illnesses. The natural self-healing and self-repairing ability that is hibernating in your body wakes up and takes over, and diseases disappear."
   > — *The End of Diabetes*, Dr. Fuhrman

#### The Biological Mechanisms

**Cumulative Damage Theory of Aging**: Modern science shows aging results from:
- Cross-linking of tissue proteins
- DNA damage (including methylation defects)
- Increase in reactive oxygen species (ROS) and superoxide radicals
- Glycation of proteins and buildup of advanced glycation end products (AGEs)

**Micronutrients prevent this damage** by:
- Neutralizing free radicals (antioxidants)
- Supporting DNA repair mechanisms
- Providing cofactors for detoxification enzymes
- Reducing inflammation

#### Why the Standard Diet Fails

The American diet:
- **62% of calories** from processed foods (micronutrient-barren)
- **25.5% of calories** from animal products (low in phytochemicals)
- **Only 10%** from vegetables, beans, seeds, nuts, and fruits
- Of that 10%, half is white potatoes (chips, fries) - leaving only **5%** from truly nutrient-rich foods

> "We could not design a better plan to prematurely kill off our population."
> — *The End of Diabetes*

#### Quality Criteria for Food

The quality of a diet can be judged on three criteria:
1. **Levels of micronutrients** (vitamins, minerals) per calorie
2. **Levels of phytochemicals** per calorie
3. **Absence of potentially harmful substances** (saturated fat, sodium, refined carbs)

#### The ANDI Score System

Dr. Fuhrman developed the Aggregate Nutrient Density Index (ANDI) to quantify this:
- Scale of 1-1000
- **Leafy greens score 1000** (highest)
- **Kale, collards, watercress = 100** on the simplified 0-100 scale
- **Processed foods score near 0**

This demonstrates that vegetables deliver the most "nutrient bang for each caloric buck."

## Part 2: G-BOMBS - The Super Foods

### The Guideline
Eat G-BOMBS every day: **G**reens, **B**eans, **O**nions, **M**ushrooms, **B**erries, **S**eeds

### The Scientific Justification

> "The foods with the strongest evidence supporting their anticancer and longevity-promoting effects are green vegetables, particularly leafy green cruciferous vegetables, followed by the other G-BOMBS."
> — *Eat for Life*

---

### G - GREENS (Cruciferous Vegetables)

**Why greens are #1:**

1. **Highest nutrient density of any food**: On ANDI scoring, leafy greens score 1000/1000. No other food comes close.

2. **Isothiocyanates (ITCs)**: Cruciferous vegetables contain unique compounds called glucosinolates. When you chew, chop, or blend these vegetables, an enzyme called **myrosinase** converts glucosinolates into **isothiocyanates** - powerful anticancer compounds.

3. **Documented Cancer Protection**:
   > "A review of more than 206 epidemiological studies shows that the consumption of raw green vegetables has the most consistent and powerful association with reduction of cancer of all types, including stomach, pancreas, colon, and breast."
   > — *Super Immunity*

4. **Why Preparation Method Matters**:
   - Raw cruciferous = maximum ITC production (myrosinase intact)
   - Cooking destroys myrosinase enzyme
   - **Solution**: Chop or blend BEFORE cooking to release ITCs while raw, then cook. The ITCs remain stable after formation.
   - Keep some cruciferous raw in salads for enzyme activity

**Specific mechanisms**:
- **Sulforaphane** (from broccoli): Induces cell cycle arrest and apoptosis in cancer cells
- ITCs detoxify carcinogens before they can damage DNA
- Block tumor growth at multiple stages

---

### B - BEANS (Legumes)

**Why beans are essential:**

1. **Resistant Starch**: Unlike refined carbs, beans contain resistant starch that:
   - Ferments in the colon, producing short-chain fatty acids (SCFAs)
   - Feeds beneficial gut bacteria
   - Slows glucose absorption dramatically

2. **Blood Sugar Stability**:
   > "Beans have a very low glycemic index and glycemic load compared to grains. They slow glucose absorption and create prolonged satiety."

   | Food | Glycemic Index | Glycemic Load |
   |------|---------------|---------------|
   | Kidney Beans | 22 | 7 |
   | Black Beans | 20 | 6 |
   | White Rice | 73 | 29 |

3. **The SCFA Mechanism**:
   - SCFAs slow glycolysis in the liver (delays hunger)
   - Increase breakdown of body fat for energy
   - Feed beneficial gut bacteria while starving harmful ones

   > "Good bacteria feast on fiber and resistant starch, while bad bacteria feast on refined sugar and animal fat."

4. **Protein Without Harm**:
   - High-quality plant protein
   - No IGF-1 stimulation (unlike animal protein)
   - Associated with lower cancer risk, not higher

5. **Economic Superstar**: Beans are the most economical nutrient-dense food available.

---

### O - ONIONS (Allium Family)

**Why onions/garlic daily:**

1. **Organosulfur Compounds**: The allium family (onions, garlic, leeks, shallots, chives, scallions) contains unique organosulfur compounds released when chopped, crushed, or chewed.

2. **Documented Benefits**:
   > "The allium family of vegetables contains anticancer, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant compounds."
   > — *Eat for Life Cookbook*

   > "Onions offer protection against diabetes, heart disease, and cancer. They can even lower cholesterol. Their powers are enhanced when eaten raw."

3. **Optimal Intake**: ½ cup chopped onions daily is considered optimal for immune function

4. **Quercetin Content**: Onions are one of the richest sources of quercetin, a flavonoid with:
   - Anti-inflammatory properties
   - Antioxidant activity
   - Cardiovascular protection

---

### M - MUSHROOMS

**Why mushrooms must be cooked:**

1. **Aromatase Inhibitors**: Mushrooms contain compounds that inhibit aromatase, an enzyme that produces estrogen. This is significant for:
   - Breast cancer prevention (estrogen-driven)
   - Reducing cancer cell proliferation

2. **Immune-Boosting Beta-Glucans**: Mushrooms contain beta-glucans that enhance immune function by activating macrophages and natural killer cells.

3. **CRITICAL: Must Be Cooked**:
   - Raw mushrooms contain a mild toxin (agaritine in button mushrooms)
   - Cooking neutralizes this while preserving beneficial compounds
   - Never eat mushrooms raw

4. **Anti-Angiogenic Properties**: Mushrooms help prevent new blood vessel formation that tumors need to grow.

---

### B - BERRIES

**Why berries are prioritized over other fruits:**

1. **Highest Antioxidant Content**: Berries have the highest ORAC (Oxygen Radical Absorbance Capacity) scores of any fruits.

2. **Anthocyanins**: The deep purple/blue pigments are powerful antioxidants that:
   - Cross the blood-brain barrier (brain protection)
   - Reduce inflammation
   - Protect against cognitive decline

3. **Lower Glycemic Impact**: Berries have lower sugar content than most fruits, making them diabetic-friendly.

4. **Cancer Prevention**: Multiple studies show berry consumption associated with reduced cancer risk, particularly:
   - Oral cancers
   - Esophageal cancers
   - Colon cancer

5. **Prioritized Berries**: Blueberries, strawberries, raspberries, blackberries, cherries, pomegranates

---

### S - SEEDS (and Nuts)

**Why seeds/nuts are required:**

1. **Essential Fatty Acids**: Seeds provide omega-3 ALA that the body needs but cannot synthesize.

2. **Fat-Soluble Nutrient Absorption**:
   > "When there is fat present in the meal, the most powerful micronutrients found in vegetables are absorbed more readily into the body."
   > — *Super Immunity*

   Eating salad without fat = poor absorption of carotenoids and other fat-soluble phytonutrients.

3. **Vitamin E & Selenium**: Seeds are rich in these antioxidants that protect cell membranes.

4. **Satiety**: The fat and protein in seeds/nuts create lasting satiety, preventing overeating.

5. **Ground Flax Specifically**:
   - Highest plant source of omega-3 ALA
   - Lignans with anticancer properties
   - Must be ground (whole seeds pass through undigested)

6. **Why Raw Matters**:
   > "Roasting destroys nutrients and creates carcinogens."
   - Eat nuts and seeds raw
   - Exception: Light toasting for flavor is acceptable, but not heavy roasting

## Part 3: Daily Requirements

### The Guidelines
- Large salad daily (1 pound raw vegetables)
- 1 pound cooked vegetables daily
- ½ cup beans/legumes
- 3+ fresh fruits (emphasize berries)
- 1 oz raw nuts/seeds
- 1 tablespoon ground flax seeds
- At least 2 servings cruciferous vegetables

### The Scientific Justification

#### Why These Specific Quantities?

1. **1 Pound Raw + 1 Pound Cooked Vegetables**:
   - This volume ensures adequate micronutrient intake
   - Raw vegetables preserve heat-sensitive nutrients (vitamin C, certain enzymes)
   - Cooked vegetables release other nutrients (lycopene, beta-carotene become more bioavailable)
   - Combination provides maximum nutrient diversity

2. **Why "Large Salad" as Foundation**:
   > "The salad is the main dish."
   > — *The End of Diabetes*

   - High volume, low calorie = satiety without excess
   - Raw vegetables provide intact enzymes and maximum vitamin content
   - Chewing raw food promotes ITC formation in cruciferous vegetables

3. **½ Cup Beans Daily**:
   - Sufficient resistant starch for gut microbiome benefits
   - Provides ~7-8g fiber per serving
   - Stabilizes blood glucose for hours after consumption
   - Economical way to meet protein needs

4. **1 oz Nuts/Seeds**:
   - Provides essential fatty acids for nutrient absorption
   - Fat required for absorption of fat-soluble vitamins (A, D, E, K) and carotenoids
   - 1 oz is the sweet spot: enough for benefits, not so much as to impede weight loss

5. **1 Tbsp Ground Flax**:
   - Provides ~2.3g ALA omega-3
   - Lignans with anticancer properties
   - Must be ground - whole seeds pass through undigested
   - Store ground flax in refrigerator (oils oxidize)

## Part 4: The 5 Deadliest Foods

### The Guidelines
1. Barbecued, processed, and cured meats
2. Fried foods
3. Full-fat dairy + Trans fats
4. Soft drinks, sugar, artificial sweeteners
5. White rice and white-flour products

### The Scientific Justification

#### 1. Processed/Cured Meats - WHY DEADLIEST

**Mechanisms of harm:**

- **Heterocyclic Amines (HCAs)**: Formed when meat is cooked at high temperatures. HCAs are documented carcinogens that damage DNA.

- **Polycyclic Aromatic Hydrocarbons (PAHs)**: Created when fat drips onto flames and smoke deposits on meat. PAHs are carcinogenic.

- **Nitrates/Nitrites**: Added to processed meats as preservatives. Convert to N-nitroso compounds in the body - potent carcinogens linked to colorectal cancer.

- **WHO Classification**: In 2015, the World Health Organization classified processed meat as a Group 1 carcinogen (same category as tobacco smoking).

**Specific risks:**
- 18% increased colorectal cancer risk per 50g daily serving
- Increased risk of stomach, pancreatic, and prostate cancers
- Higher all-cause mortality

#### 2. Fried Foods - WHY DEADLY

**Mechanisms of harm:**

- **Acrylamide Formation**: When starchy foods are fried at high temperatures, acrylamide (a probable carcinogen) forms.

- **Oxidized Oils**: Reheated oils (common in commercial frying) contain oxidized lipids and aldehydes that promote inflammation and cardiovascular disease.

- **Advanced Glycation End Products (AGEs)**: High-heat cooking creates AGEs that accelerate aging and diabetes complications.

- **Trans Fats**: Partial hydrogenation of oils (still used in some commercial frying) creates trans fats - proven to increase heart disease risk.

#### 3. Full-Fat Dairy + Trans Fats - WHY HARMFUL

**Dairy mechanisms:**
- High in saturated fat (promotes cardiovascular disease)
- Contains IGF-1 (linked to cancer cell proliferation)
- Casein protein linked to cancer promotion in animal studies
- Lactose intolerance affects majority of world population

**Trans fats:**
- No safe level of consumption
- Raise LDL ("bad") cholesterol
- Lower HDL ("good") cholesterol
- Increase inflammation
- Promote insulin resistance

#### 4. Soft Drinks, Sugar, Artificial Sweeteners - WHY HARMFUL

**Sugar mechanisms:**
- Rapid blood glucose spikes → insulin surges → fat storage
- Promotes insulin resistance over time
- Feeds pathogenic gut bacteria
- Provides zero micronutrients (empty calories)
- Addictive - activates same brain pathways as drugs

**Artificial sweeteners:**
- Disrupt gut microbiome
- May still trigger insulin response
- Maintain preference for sweet taste (don't help break addiction)

> "No sweetened drinks of any type are permitted, even artificially sweetened. Even no-calorie sweeteners can stimulate the pancreas to work."
> — *The End of Diabetes*

#### 5. White Rice/White Flour - WHY HARMFUL

**Mechanisms:**
- Fiber and nutrients stripped during processing
- Rapid glucose absorption (high glycemic index)
- White rice glycemic index: 73 vs kidney beans: 22
- No resistant starch benefits
- Promotes the same metabolic dysfunction as sugar

## Part 5: The 90/10 Rule

### The Guideline
- 90%+ calories from nutrient-dense plant foods
- <10% from animal products + processed foods combined

### The Scientific Justification

This ratio is not arbitrary - it's based on:

1. **Micronutrient Sufficiency**: At 90% plant foods, you achieve adequate intake of all protective phytochemicals. Below this, deficiencies accumulate.

2. **Caloric Density**: Plant foods (especially vegetables) are low in caloric density, making overeating nearly impossible at 90%.

3. **IGF-1 Modulation**: Animal protein stimulates IGF-1 (insulin-like growth factor 1). Keeping animal products below 10% maintains healthy IGF-1 levels.

4. **Flexibility**: The 10% allows for social eating and personal preference without undermining the diet's effectiveness.

5. **Disease Reversal Threshold**: Dr. Fuhrman's clinical experience shows that disease reversal (diabetes, heart disease) requires this threshold to be met.

## Part 9: Oils - Why Forbidden

### The Guideline
No added oil of any type - not olive oil, coconut oil, or any cooking oil.

### The Scientific Justification

> "Oil is fattening, containing 120 calories per tablespoon, and can sabotage your opportunity to lose weight or reverse your diabetes. Plus, it does not have the protective effects on your heart or diabetes. This program uses seeds and nuts, not oil, as the main fat source."
> — *The End of Diabetes*

**Why whole foods over extracted oils:**

1. **Caloric Density**: Oil is the most calorically dense food (120 cal/tbsp). It provides calories without fiber, protein, or micronutrients.

2. **Nutrient Loss**: When oil is extracted from olives, nuts, or seeds:
   - Fiber is removed
   - Protein is removed
   - Many phytochemicals are lost
   - Only fat remains

3. **The Mediterranean Diet Misunderstanding**:
   > "The beneficial effects of the Mediterranean diet are not due to the consumption of olive oil; they are due to antioxidant-rich foods, including vegetables, fruits, and beans, as well as lots of onions and garlic."

4. **Whole Food Alternative**:
   - Instead of olive oil → eat olives
   - Instead of coconut oil → eat coconut (sparingly)
   - Instead of oil dressings → nut/seed-based dressings

5. **Fat Absorption**: You still need fat for nutrient absorption. The diet provides this through:
   - Nuts and seeds (1 oz daily)
   - Avocado
   - Whole olives

## Part 10: Salt/Sodium Limits

### The Guideline
- Men: <1,200 mg/day
- Women: <1,000 mg/day
- Natural foods contain 400-700 mg
- Added sodium allowed: only 200-400 mg extra

### The Scientific Justification

**Why such strict limits (stricter than mainstream advice):**

1. **Blood Pressure**: Salt causes water retention and increases blood pressure. Even "normal" blood pressure is elevated compared to populations eating truly low-salt diets.

2. **Stomach Cancer**: High salt intake damages stomach lining and promotes H. pylori colonization, both risk factors for stomach cancer.

3. **Calcium Loss**: Salt increases calcium excretion through urine, contributing to osteoporosis over time.

4. **Taste Bud Adaptation**:
   > "Salt deadens taste buds. Removing it restores flavor appreciation."

   After 2-3 weeks without added salt, natural food flavors become vivid and satisfying.

5. **The Addiction Cycle**: Like sugar, salt creates tolerance. You need more and more to achieve the same taste satisfaction.

**Practical implications:**
- Sea salt is NOT healthier (still 98% sodium chloride)
- Canned foods are major sodium sources
- Restaurant food typically contains 2-3x daily sodium limit in one meal

## Part 11: Required Supplements

### The Guidelines

| Supplement | Why Needed | Dose |
|------------|------------|------|
| **Vitamin D** | Many are deficient, especially without sun exposure | 1000-2000 IU (per blood test) |
| **Vitamin B12** | Essential for vegans/near-vegans; absorption declines with age | 250-500 mcg/day |
| **Zinc** | Difficult to get enough on plant-based diet; critical for immunity | ~15 mg |
| **Iodine** | Needed especially if avoiding iodized salt | ~150 mcg |
| **DHA (algae)** | Omega-3 for brain/immune function | 100-200 mg/day |

### Supplements to AVOID:
- Vitamin A (retinyl acetate/palmitate)
- Folic Acid (synthetic)
- Beta-carotene (isolated)
- Copper
- Iron (for men and post-menopausal women)

### The Scientific Justification

#### Why These Five Are Necessary

**1. Vitamin D:**
- Not truly a vitamin - it's a hormone the body produces from sunlight
- Modern indoor lifestyles create widespread deficiency
- Critical for immune function, bone health, and cancer prevention
- Blood levels should be tested and maintained at 30-50 ng/mL

**2. Vitamin B12:**
- Produced by bacteria, not plants or animals
- Historically obtained from soil residue on plants
- Modern sanitation eliminates this source
- Deficiency causes irreversible neurological damage
- Absorption decreases with age (even meat-eaters become deficient)

**3. Zinc:**
- Plant foods contain zinc, but phytates reduce absorption
- Critical for immune cell function
- Deficiency impairs wound healing and increases infection risk
- The diet may provide insufficient absorbable zinc

**4. Iodine:**
- Sea vegetables are the plant source, but most people don't eat them
- If avoiding iodized salt (as the diet recommends), supplementation needed
- Essential for thyroid function

**5. DHA (Docosahexaenoic Acid):**
- Long-chain omega-3 essential for brain and eye health
- Body can convert ALA (from flax) to DHA, but conversion is inefficient
- Conversion rate varies by individual (some convert almost none)
- Men convert less than women
- Conversion declines with age
- Algae-derived DHA is pure (no mercury/pollutants unlike fish oil)

#### Why Avoid Certain Supplements

**Vitamin A (retinyl forms):**
- Studies show 16% increased mortality with supplementation
- Beta-carotene from food is safe; the body converts only what it needs
- Supplemental vitamin A bypasses this regulation

**Folic Acid (synthetic):**
- Linked to increased cancer risk in studies
- Folate from greens is the natural form - the diet provides abundant folate
- Synthetic folic acid may accumulate and have different effects

**Isolated Beta-Carotene:**
- Studies show increased lung cancer risk in smokers
- Whole food carotenoids are protective; isolated supplements are not

**Copper:**
- Most diets provide excess copper
- Supplemental copper may impair immune function

**Iron (for men/post-menopausal women):**
- Iron is an oxidant (promotes free radical damage)
- Excess iron linked to increased heart attack risk
- The diet provides adequate iron from plant sources

## Part 12: Omega-3 Protocol

### The Guidelines

| Source | Type | Recommendation |
|--------|------|----------------|
| Ground flax seeds | ALA | 1 tablespoon daily |
| Walnuts | ALA | Include regularly |
| Chia/hemp seeds | ALA | Include regularly |
| Leafy greens | ALA | Already eating lots |
| Algae-derived DHA | DHA | 100-200 mg/day supplement |

### The Scientific Justification

#### The Omega-3 Family

There are three important omega-3 fatty acids:

1. **ALA (Alpha-linolenic acid)**: Found in plants (flax, chia, walnuts). The body can convert to EPA and DHA.

2. **EPA (Eicosapentaenoic acid)**: Anti-inflammatory, cardiovascular benefits

3. **DHA (Docosahexaenoic acid)**: Critical for brain structure, retina, cell membranes

#### Why Supplement DHA

The conversion pathway: ALA → EPA → DHA

**The Problem:**
- Conversion efficiency is highly variable (2-15% typically)
- Men convert less than women (estrogen enhances conversion)
- Elderly convert less than young people
- High omega-6 intake inhibits conversion (most Americans eat too much omega-6)
- Some individuals have genetic variants that impair conversion

**The Solution:**
- Eat ample ALA sources (flax, chia, walnuts) for what conversion does occur
- Supplement with preformed DHA to ensure adequacy
- Use algae-derived DHA (where fish get their DHA originally)

#### Why NOT Fish

> "Limited or no fish recommended - pollution concerns (mercury, PCBs, dioxins)"

- Fish accumulate toxins from polluted waters
- Mercury is a neurotoxin
- PCBs and dioxins are carcinogenic
- Farm-raised tilapia has NO DHA
- Large predatory fish have highest toxin loads

If eating fish: avoid shark, swordfish, mackerel, pike, bluefish (highest mercury)

## Part 13: Exercise Requirements

### The Guidelines
- Daily exercise recommended
- Vigorous exercise at least 3 days/week (heart rate elevated for 5+ minutes)
- Strength training for legs and core

### The Scientific Justification

> "No dietary approach will succeed without attention to other risk factors - the main ones being a sedentary lifestyle, smoking, and lack of sleep."

**Why Exercise is Non-Negotiable:**

1. **Insulin Sensitivity**: Exercise directly improves insulin sensitivity, helping reverse diabetes. Muscle contractions move glucose into cells without insulin.

2. **Cardiovascular Health**: Heart is a muscle that needs training. Regular exercise reduces blood pressure and improves lipid profiles.

3. **Weight Maintenance**: Exercise preserves muscle mass during weight loss. Muscle is metabolically active tissue that burns calories at rest.

4. **Mental Health**: Exercise releases endorphins and reduces depression/anxiety that can sabotage dietary compliance.

5. **Longevity**: Consistent moderate exercise is one of the strongest predictors of lifespan.

**Why Vigorous Exercise:**
- "Vigorous" means heart rate significantly elevated
- Minimum 5+ minutes continuous
- Examples: jogging, cycling, swimming, jumping
- This level triggers cardiovascular adaptations that moderate exercise doesn't

**Warning About Extreme Exercise:**
> "Extreme exercise (marathons, triathlons) can produce excessive free radicals"

Moderate-vigorous is optimal, not extreme. Ultra-endurance activities create oxidative stress that may offset benefits.

## Part 15: Diabetics Protocol

### The Guidelines
- Eliminate white potatoes, white rice, white bread, white pasta
- Favor beans over whole grains
- Sweet potatoes/butternut squash preferred over white potatoes
- Edamame - exceptionally low carb, excellent for diabetics

### The Scientific Justification

**Why Beans Over Grains for Diabetics:**

> "Diabetics need the opposite [of the standard diet]: a diet high in vegetables and beans and low in animal products."

1. **Glycemic Load Comparison:**
   - Kidney beans: GL of 7
   - Black beans: GL of 6

## Part 7: Cruciferous Vegetables - Preparation Methods

### The Guidelines
1. Chew thoroughly OR blend/chop BEFORE cooking
2. Undercook slightly - don't make mushy
3. Add raw chopped cruciferous to salads

### The Scientific Justification

**The Myrosinase-Isothiocyanate System:**

This is critical to understand:

1. Cruciferous vegetables contain **glucosinolates** (stored in cell vacuoles)
2. They also contain an enzyme called **myrosinase** (stored in different cell compartments)
3. When cells are broken (chewing, chopping, blending), glucosinolates meet myrosinase
4. Myrosinase converts glucosinolates into **isothiocyanates (ITCs)** - the anticancer compounds

**The Cooking Problem:**
- Heat destroys myrosinase enzyme
- If you cook cruciferous vegetables whole, then chew them, no ITCs are formed
- The glucosinolates pass through without conversion

**The Solutions:**

1. **Eat some raw**: Raw cruciferous in salads ensures ITC formation

2. **Chop/blend BEFORE cooking**:
   > "If you crush, chop, or juice the greens while they're raw and then put the blended or chopped greens into a stew or soup to cook, you will still have those functioning and beneficial compounds present after cooking."

   Once ITCs are formed, they're heat-stable.

3. **Undercook**: Shorter cooking times preserve more myrosinase activity

**Maximum Steaming Times:**
- Broccoli: 5-7 minutes
- Kale/Collards: 5-7 minutes
- Brussels sprouts: 6-8 minutes
- Cabbage: 5-7 minutes
