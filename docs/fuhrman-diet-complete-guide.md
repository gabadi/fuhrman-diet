## PART 1: CORE PRINCIPLE

### The Health Equation

**H = N/C** (Health = Nutrients / Calories)

Your health is predicted by your nutrient intake divided by your caloric intake. Maximize micronutrient density per calorie consumed.

## PART 3: DAILY REQUIREMENTS (Non-Negotiable)

### Include Every Day:
1. **Large salad** - foundation of the diet
2. **1/2 cup beans/legumes** - in soup, salad, or dish
3. **At least 3 fresh fruits** - emphasize berries, pomegranates, cherries, plums, oranges
4. **At least 1 oz raw nuts/seeds** - (limit to 1 oz if losing weight)
5. **Double-serving cooked green vegetables** - steamed, in soups/stews
6. **1 tablespoon ground flax seeds** - for omega-3 ALA

### Target Vegetable Intake:
- **1 pound raw vegetables daily**
- **1 pound cooked vegetables daily**
- **At least 2 servings cruciferous vegetables** (at least 1 raw)
- **8 total servings vegetables** + **6 fresh fruits**

## PART 7: CRUCIFEROUS VEGETABLES (Prioritize These)

Arugula, Bok choy, Broccoli, Broccoli rabe, Brussels sprouts, Cabbage, Cauliflower, Collard greens, Horseradish, Kale, Kohlrabi, Mustard greens, Radishes, Red cabbage, Rutabaga leaf, Swiss chard, Turnip greens, Watercress

### How to Maximize Cruciferous Benefits:
1. **Chew thoroughly** or **blend/chop BEFORE cooking** (releases isothiocyanates)
2. **Undercook slightly** - don't make mushy
3. Add raw chopped cruciferous to salads for enzyme activity

## PART 10: SALT/SODIUM - STRICT LIMITS

| Population | Daily Sodium Limit |
|------------|-------------------|
| Men | **< 1,200 mg/day** |
| Women | **< 1,000 mg/day** |
| Natural foods contain | 400-700 mg |
| Added sodium allowed | Only 200-400 mg extra |

### Why so strict:
- High salt = higher blood pressure, stroke, heart disease
- Salt promotes stomach cancer
- Salt causes calcium loss leading to osteoporosis
- Salt deadens taste buds (removing it restores flavor appreciation)

### Practical tips:
- Use unsalted canned beans (rinse if salted)
- Season with garlic, onion, herbs, spices, lemon, vinegar
- Read labels - canned soups are sodium bombs
- Sea salt is NOT healthier (still 98% sodium chloride)

## PART 11: REQUIRED SUPPLEMENTS

Dr. Fuhrman explicitly recommends these for almost everyone:

| Supplement | Why Needed | Dose |
|------------|------------|------|
| **Vitamin D** | Many people are deficient, especially those not in sun | Per blood test; typically 1000-2000 IU |
| **Vitamin B12** | Essential for vegans/near-vegans; declines with age | 250-500 mcg/day |
| **Zinc** | Difficult to get enough on plant-based diet; critical for immunity | ~15 mg (RDI) |
| **Iodine** | Needed especially if avoiding iodized salt | ~150 mcg |
| **DHA (from algae)** | Omega-3 for brain/immune function; conversion from ALA varies by individual | **100-200 mg/day** |

### Supplements to AVOID:
- **Vitamin A (retinyl acetate/palmitate)** - increases cancer risk (16% increased mortality)
- **Folic Acid (synthetic)** - increases cancer risk; get folate from greens instead
- **Beta-carotene (isolated)** - increases lung cancer risk
- **Copper** - may reduce immune function
- **Iron** (for men and post-menopausal women) - oxidant, can increase heart attack risk

> **Key insight**: Choose a multivitamin **without** vitamin A, folic acid, copper, or iron. Get these from food instead.

## PART 12: OMEGA-3 FATS - COMPLETE PROTOCOL

| Source | Type | Recommendation |
|--------|------|----------------|
| **Ground flax seeds** | ALA | **1 tablespoon daily** |
| **Walnuts** | ALA | Include regularly |
| **Chia seeds, hemp seeds** | ALA | Include regularly |
| **Leafy greens** | ALA | Already eating lots |
| **Algae-derived DHA** | DHA | **100-200 mg/day supplement** |

### Why supplement DHA:
- Conversion from ALA to DHA varies by individual
- Men convert less than women
- Conversion ability declines with age
- Algae-derived DHA is clean (no mercury/pollutants like fish)

### Fish position:
- **Limited or no fish recommended** - pollution concerns (mercury, PCBs, dioxins)
- If eating fish: avoid shark, swordfish, mackerel, pike, bluefish (highest mercury)
- Farm-raised tilapia has NO DHA
