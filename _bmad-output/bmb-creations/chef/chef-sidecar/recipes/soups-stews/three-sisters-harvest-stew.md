# Three Sisters Harvest Stew

> Source: Eat to Live Cookbook

## Quick Info
- **Servings:** 4
- **Time:** 35 min
- **Category:** soups-stews
- **Kosher:** Parve
- **G-BOMBS:** Greens, Beans, Onions, Seeds

## Ingredients
- 1 medium onion, chopped
- 2 cloves garlic, chopped
- 1 jalapeño chili, seeded and minced
- 1 1/2 cups cooked kidney or pinto beans (or 1 can no-salt-added, drained)
- 1/2 cup green bell pepper, cut into strips
- 1/2 cup red bell pepper, cut into strips
- 2 cups corn kernels
- 2 cups low-sodium or no-salt-added vegetable stock
- 3 cups chopped acorn, butternut, or carnival squash, peeled and cut into 3/4-inch chunks
- 1 teaspoon ground cumin
- 1 1/2 cups diced tomatoes, fresh or BPA-free cartons
- 1 teaspoon dried oregano
- 1/8 teaspoon ground pepper
- 5 ounces spinach
- 1/4 cup raw pumpkin seeds, lightly toasted

## Instructions
1. Heat 2 tablespoons water in a soup pot, add the onion, and sauté until tender.
2. Add the garlic and jalapeño and continue to sauté for an additional minute.
3. Add remaining ingredients except spinach and pumpkin seeds.
4. Bring to a boil, reduce heat, and simmer until all the vegetables are tender, about 25 minutes.
5. Add additional vegetable broth if needed to adjust consistency.
6. Stir in spinach and heat until wilted.
7. Serve topped with toasted pumpkin seeds.
