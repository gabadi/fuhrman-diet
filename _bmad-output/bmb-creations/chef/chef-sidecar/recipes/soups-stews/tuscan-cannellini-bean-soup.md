# Tuscan Cannellini Bean Soup with Chiffonade of Collard Greens

> Source: Eat to Live Cookbook

## Quick Info
- **Servings:** 6
- **Time:** 45 min (plus 1 hour soak for dried tomatoes)
- **Category:** soups-stews
- **Kosher:** Parve
- **G-BOMBS:** Greens, Beans, Onions

## Ingredients
- 1 cup unsulfured, unsalted dried tomatoes
- 1/2 cup unsweetened soy, hemp, or almond milk
- 1 cup finely chopped onions
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 tablespoon fresh oregano
- 1 tablespoon fresh rosemary
- 1 1/2 cups cooked cannellini beans (or 1 can no-salt-added, drained)
- 1 1/2 cups diced tomatoes, fresh or BPA-free cartons
- 1 1/2 cups no-salt-added tomato puree
- 3 cups no-salt-added or low-sodium vegetable broth
- 1 cup water
- 1 tablespoon Dr. Fuhrman's MatoZest or no-salt seasoning blend
- 6 cups collard greens, cut chiffonade (very thinly sliced)
- 2 tablespoons sherry vinegar or Black Fig Vinegar
- 1/2 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
- 2 tablespoons fresh basil

## Instructions
1. Soak the dried tomatoes in soy milk for 1 hour to soften. Drain, reserving soy milk. Chop tomatoes finely and set aside.
2. Water-saut√© onions and garlic in a large pot, stirring constantly.
3. Add dried tomatoes, reserved soy milk, oregano, rosemary, beans, diced tomatoes, tomato puree, broth, water, and MatoZest.
4. Bring to a boil. Lower heat and simmer for about 20 minutes.
5. Add the sliced greens just before serving and bring to a simmer, no longer than 2 minutes.
6. Before serving, stir in vinegar, black pepper, and fresh basil.
