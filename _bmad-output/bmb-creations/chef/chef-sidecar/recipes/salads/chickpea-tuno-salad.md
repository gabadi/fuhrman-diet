# Chickpea "Tuno" Salad

> Source: Eat to Live Cookbook (Chef James Rohrbacher)

## Quick Info
- **Servings:** 5
- **Time:** 40 min
- **Category:** salads
- **Kosher:** Parve
- **G-BOMBS:** Beans, Onions

## Ingredients
- 3 cups cooked chickpeas or 2 (15 ounce) cans no-salt-added or low-sodium chickpeas, drained
- 1/2 cup raw almonds, preferably blanched
- 2 tablespoons lemon juice, or more to taste
- 1 teaspoon kelp granules
- 1 (12.3-ounce) package firm lite silken tofu
- 2 tablespoons white wine or champagne vinegar
- 1 teaspoon dry mustard powder
- 2 tablespoons nutritional yeast
- 2 teaspoons Dijon mustard
- 2 medium celery stalks, diced
- 3 green onions, minced
- 1/4 cup red bell pepper, minced
- 3/4 cup frozen peas, thawed
- Freshly ground black pepper

## Instructions
1. In a food processor, pulse the chickpeas and almonds until coarsely chopped.
2. Add the lemon juice and kelp powder and pulse a few more times. Transfer to a large mixing bowl.
3. Place the tofu, vinegar, dry mustard, yeast, and mustard in a high-powered blender and blend until very smooth.
4. Add to the mixing bowl with the chickpea mixture, along with the celery, green onions, red pepper, peas, and black pepper. Mix thoroughly.
5. Cover and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes to let the flavors mingle before serving.

## Nutrition (per serving)
Calories: 358 | Protein: 19g | Carbohydrates: 37g | Total Fat: 16.2g | Fiber: 13.1g

## Notes
- This easy-to-make mock tuna salad is delicious and convenient to take to work placed on top of a green salad
- Almonds can be blanched to remove the skins - place them in boiling water for 1 minute, drain, and rinse with cold water, then squeeze to slide the skin off
