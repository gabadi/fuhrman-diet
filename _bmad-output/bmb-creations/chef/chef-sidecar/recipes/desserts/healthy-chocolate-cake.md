# Healthy Chocolate Cake

> Source: Eat to Live Cookbook

## Quick Info
- **Servings:** 16
- **Time:** 75 min
- **Category:** desserts
- **Kosher:** Parve
- **G-BOMBS:** None

## Ingredients

### For the Cake:
- 1 2/3 cups whole-wheat flour
- 1 teaspoon baking powder
- 3 teaspoons baking soda
- 3 1/2 cups pitted dates, divided
- 1 cup pineapple chunks in own juice, drained
- 1 banana
- 1/2 cup unsweetened applesauce
- 1/2 cup shredded beets
- 1/2 cup shredded carrots
- 1/2 cup shredded zucchini
- 4 tablespoons natural cocoa powder
- 1/2 cup currants
- 1/2 cup chopped walnuts
- 2 cups water
- 2 teaspoons vanilla extract

### For the Chocolate Nut Icing:
- 1/2 cup raw macadamia nuts or raw cashews, unsalted
- 1 cup vanilla soy, hemp, or almond milk
- 2/3 cup pitted dates
- 1/3 cup Brazil nuts or hazelnuts
- 2 tablespoons natural cocoa powder
- 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

## Instructions
1. Preheat the oven to 350Â°F. Mix flour, baking powder, and baking soda in a small bowl. Set aside.
2. In a blender or food processor, puree 3 cups of the dates, pineapple, banana, and applesauce.
3. Slice remaining 1/2 cup dates into 1/2-inch-thick pieces.
4. In a large bowl, mix sliced dates, beets, carrots, zucchini, cocoa powder, currants, walnuts, water, vanilla, and flour mixture.
5. Add the blended mixture and mix well.
6. Spread in a 9 1/2 x 13 1/2-inch nonstick baking pan.
7. Bake for 1 hour or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.
8. For the chocolate nut icing, combine all ingredients in a high-powered blender until smooth and creamy.
9. Place a dollop over warm cake and serve or spread on cooled cake.

## Notes
- To make individual servings, bake in muffin pans lined with paper liners. Reduce cooking time to 20-25 minutes.
- Contains beets - user dislikes beets, may want to reduce or omit.
