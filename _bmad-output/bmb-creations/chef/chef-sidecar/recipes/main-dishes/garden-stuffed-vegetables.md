# Garden-Stuffed Vegetables

> Source: Eat to Live Cookbook

## Quick Info
- **Servings:** 6
- **Time:** 75 min
- **Category:** main-dishes
- **Kosher:** Parve
- **G-BOMBS:** Greens, Beans, Mushrooms, Onions

## Ingredients
- 2 medium zucchini, cut in half lengthwise, seeds and some meat removed
- 4 large bell peppers, assorted colors, tops sliced off, seeds removed
- 2 medium portobello mushrooms, stems removed
- 1/2 cup quinoa, rinsed well
- 1 small red bell pepper, chopped
- 1/2 pound shiitake mushrooms, chopped
- 4 whole green onions, chopped
- 2 stalks celery, chopped
- 1 stalk broccoli, chopped in small pieces
- 4 cloves garlic, chopped
- 1/2 cup cooked lentils or canned no-salt-added lentils
- 1/4 cup walnuts, coarsely chopped
- 3 ounces nondairy, mozzarella-type cheese, shredded
- 1/2 cup raisins (reduce to 1/4 cup for weight-loss diets)
- 1/4 cup plus 2 tablespoons parsley, chopped, divided
- 2 teaspoons Bragg Liquid Aminos
- No-salt seasoning blend or 1 tablespoon Dr. Fuhrman's MatoZest
- 2 cups low-sodium pasta sauce
- Salad greens
- 1 tablespoon champagne vinegar

## Instructions
1. Preheat the oven to 350°F.
2. On a baking sheet, bake the zucchini and peppers for 5 minutes. Add portobello mushrooms and bake for an additional 15 minutes. Set aside.
3. Rinse the quinoa and place in a saucepot with 1 cup water. Bring to a boil, cover, reduce heat, and simmer for 13 to 15 minutes, until water is absorbed.
4. In a small amount of water, sauté red pepper, shiitake mushrooms, green onions, celery, broccoli, and garlic until tender.
5. In a large bowl, mix cooked quinoa, lentils, walnuts, cheese substitute, raisins, and 1/4 cup chopped parsley with sautéed ingredients. Season with liquid aminos and MatoZest.
6. Fill zucchini, mushrooms, and peppers with quinoa mixture and place in a baking dish.
7. Spoon pasta sauce over vegetables.
8. Bake for 20 to 30 minutes until hot.
9. Serve on a bed of salad greens tossed with champagne vinegar.
10. Garnish vegetables with remaining chopped parsley.
