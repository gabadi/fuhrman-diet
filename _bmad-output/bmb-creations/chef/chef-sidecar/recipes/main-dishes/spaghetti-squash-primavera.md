# Spaghetti Squash Primavera

> Source: Eat to Live Cookbook

## Quick Info
- **Servings:** 4
- **Time:** 75 min
- **Category:** main-dishes
- **Kosher:** Parve
- **G-BOMBS:** Greens, Beans, Onions

## Ingredients
- 1 medium spaghetti squash
- 1 1/2 carrots, diagonally sliced
- 1/2 cup diagonally sliced celery
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 1/2 cups shredded cabbage
- 1 small zucchini, chopped into small pieces
- 1 1/2 cups cooked pinto beans or 1 (15-ounce) can low-sodium pinto beans, drained
- 1 1/2 cups chopped tomatoes, fresh or packaged
- 1/3 cup low-sodium vegetable broth
- 1 teaspoon dried thyme
- 2 tablespoons chopped fresh parsley
- 1 cup low-sodium pasta sauce
- Nutritarian "Parmesan" (optional)

## Instructions
1. Preheat the oven to 350Â°F.
2. Slice spaghetti squash in half lengthwise and remove seeds. Place both halves upside down on a baking sheet.
3. Bake for 45 minutes or until tender.
4. Meanwhile, cook carrots and celery in 2 tablespoons water in a covered pan over medium heat for 10 minutes, stirring occasionally.
5. Add garlic, cabbage, and zucchini and cook, covered, for another 10 minutes.
6. Stir in pinto beans, tomatoes, broth, thyme, and parsley. Cover and simmer for 10 minutes or until carrots are tender.
7. When squash is done, using a fork, scrape spaghetti-like strands from squash into a bowl.
8. Add pasta sauce and combine by mixing thoroughly.
9. Mix the vegetables, beans, and herbs with the squash/pasta sauce mixture.
10. Serve on a bed of shredded romaine lettuce, if desired. Sprinkle with Nutritarian "Parmesan."
