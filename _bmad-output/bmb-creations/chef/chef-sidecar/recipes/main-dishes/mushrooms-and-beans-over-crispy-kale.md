# Mushrooms and Beans over Crispy Kale

> Source: Eat to Live Cookbook

## Quick Info
- **Servings:** 2
- **Time:** 40 min
- **Category:** main-dishes
- **Kosher:** Parve
- **G-BOMBS:** Greens, Beans, Mushrooms, Onions

## Ingredients

### For the Mushrooms and Beans:
- Small amount of olive oil
- 2-4 garlic cloves, chopped
- 1 shallot, chopped
- 1 pound mixed mushrooms (portobello, cremini, shiitake), sliced or quartered
- 1 cup cooked kidney beans or canned no-salt-added kidney beans, drained
- 1/2 cup low-sodium vegetable broth
- 1 tablespoon sherry vinegar or balsamic vinegar
- 1/2 teaspoon fresh thyme, chopped
- 1/8 teaspoon black pepper

### For the Sauce:
- 1 1/4 cups low-sodium vegetable broth, divided
- 1 tablespoon tomato paste
- 1/4 cup raisins (reduce to 2 tablespoons for weight-loss diets)
- 2 teaspoons arrowroot powder

### For the Crispy Kale:
- Small amount of olive oil
- 1 bunch kale, tough stems and center ribs removed, chopped

## Instructions
1. Preheat the oven to 350Â°F.
2. For the crispy kale: Rub a baking dish with a small amount of olive oil. Spread kale leaves on the baking pan, making sure not to overlap. Bake for 20 minutes until the edges start to get crispy.
3. For the mushrooms: Rub a large nonstick skillet with olive oil and heat over medium-high heat. Add garlic, shallots, and mushrooms and cook until mushrooms are tender and liquid is evaporated.
4. Add kidney beans and vegetable broth and cook until heated through.
5. Stir in vinegar, thyme, and black pepper. Cover and set aside.
6. For the sauce: Heat 1 cup of the vegetable broth in small saucepan. Add tomato paste and raisins and simmer on low heat for 3 minutes.
7. Whisk arrowroot into remaining 1/4 cup vegetable broth. Add to sauce and heat until mixture is slightly thickened.
8. Serve mushrooms and kidney beans on a bed of crispy kale topped with sauce.
